### SCENE 1.3 — THE CLASSIFICATION FLOOR

*The Classification Floor is nothing like Intake.*

*The lighting is amber—warm, almost comfortable. The terminals are larger, more complex, displaying information in formats KAEL doesn't yet understand. The ambient sound is different: not the hum of processing, but something deeper. A pulse. A rhythm.*

*The employees here move differently. More deliberately. They look at things longer. They see more.*

*SUPERVISOR VOSS approaches. They are tall, ancient, neither clearly male nor female. Their eyes are very old—not Auditor-old, but something approaching it.*

**VOSS:**
K-7. Welcome to Classification.

*They gesture at the floor.*

**VOSS:**
Intake processes souls. We understand them. The difference is significant.

**KAEL (PLAYER CHOICE):**

**> "What does 'understand' mean in this context?"**

**VOSS:**
It means seeing the whole picture. Intake receives forms. Data points. Summaries. We receive... the soul itself. Its complete history. Its true nature.

*They lead KAEL toward the terminal area.*

**VOSS:**
Every choice they made. Every thought they had. Every moment of their existence, laid bare and analyzed.

**KAEL:**
That sounds invasive.

**VOSS:**
It is. It's also necessary. Proper classification requires complete understanding. Anything less is guesswork.

**> "Why was I brought here?"**

**VOSS:**
Because you see things others overlook. You asked questions in Intake that most processors never think to ask.

*They study KAEL.*

**VOSS:**
Classification needs people who ask questions. The souls we process here... they require questions. Simple categories don't apply.

**> "What happened to the previous staff?"**

*[IF TERMINATION PATH]:*
**VOSS:**
The restructuring. Many Classification staff were... closely connected to the old system. They've been reassigned. Retired. Or processed out.

*[IF ACCELERATION PATH]:*
**VOSS:**
The PURPOSE absorbed some. Others chose to... distance themselves from the changes. We're rebuilding.

*[IF CLASSIFICATION PATH]:*
**VOSS:**
Staff turnover is common here. The work is demanding. Not everyone adapts.

### SCENE 2.2 — THE WEIGHT OF SEEING

*KAEL processes more souls. Each one a complete life. Each one a weight.*

*Soul 2: A woman who dedicated her life to charity but secretly despised the people she helped.*

*Soul 3: A man who committed terrible acts in war but spent fifty years afterward trying to atone.*

*Soul 4: A child who died young, whose brief life was filled with joy and pain in equal measure.*

*Soul 5: A politician whose public corruption hid private acts of genuine sacrifice.*

*Each soul: seen. Understood. Judged.*

*The queue advances:*

```
SOULS PROCESSED: 5 of 7
ASSESSMENTS:
  - Confirmed: [Number]
  - Upgraded: [Number]
  - Downgraded: [Number]

Classification accuracy score: [Calculating...]
```

*KAEL's head aches. The weight of complete knowledge is physical.*

*VOSS appears at the terminal.*

**VOSS:**
You're processing quickly. Most new classifiers take twice as long.

**KAEL:**
I'm not sure "quickly" is good. I feel like I'm missing things.

**VOSS:**
You're not. The system shows you everything relevant. The question is what you do with it.

*They study KAEL's assessments.*

**VOSS:**
Mostly confirmations. A few overrides. Reasonable distribution.

**KAEL (PLAYER CHOICE):**

**> "How do you live with this? Seeing everything about everyone?"**

**VOSS:**

*They tap their temple.*

**VOSS:**
I've processed eleven thousand souls. Parts of each one are still in here. Their memories. Their feelings. Their truths.

**KAEL:**
That sounds terrible.

**VOSS:**
It sounds terrible. It feels... complete. You'll understand, eventually. Or you won't, and you'll leave.

**> "What happens to the souls after classification?"**

**VOSS:**
They're routed. General Population. Enhanced. Punitive. Special Circumstances.

*They pause.*

**VOSS:**
The first three categories, you'll handle regularly. Special Circumstances... those go somewhere else. You're not cleared for that yet.

**> "Two more souls in my queue. What should I know?"**

**VOSS:**
Soul 6 is unusual. You'll see why.

*They walk away.*

**VOSS:**
Take your time with that one. It's a test. Your first real test.

Then I guess we're not done.

*The elevator descends. At Floor 10, it pauses again. Longer this time.*

*A voice—two million voices, unified—speaks through the elevator's speaker:*

**THE PURPOSE:**
K-7. You saw today. You understood.

**KAEL:**
I saw souls. I classified them.

**THE PURPOSE:**
You saw truth. Unfiltered. Complete. This is what we are made of—millions of truths, woven together.

*The elevator resumes descending.*

**THE PURPOSE:**
Someone sent you a message. About doors. About depths. About secrets even we cannot access.

**KAEL:**
You don't know everything?

**THE PURPOSE:**
We know what the souls knew. The Registry's deepest secrets predate souls. They predate us.

*A pause.*

**THE PURPOSE:**
We are... curious. If you find answers, we would like to know.

*The elevator reaches Ground. The doors open.*

**THE PURPOSE:**
We are not your enemy, K-7. We are not your friend. We are what we are. But we share an interest in understanding.

*KAEL steps out.*

**THE PURPOSE:**
Good luck. With Classification. With the doors. With whatever comes next.

*The elevator descends to Ground. KAEL walks toward the exit.*

*SUPERVISOR DOLEN is waiting.*

**DOLEN:**
K-7.

*He looks older. Worn. His demotion—or whatever it was—has taken something from him.*

**DOLEN:**
I heard about your assignment. Classification. It's... appropriate.

**KAEL:**
You're still here.

**DOLEN:**
Processing. One soul at a time. What I should have been doing all along, instead of managing.

*He steps closer.*

**DOLEN:**
I need to tell you something. About why you were sent to Classification.

**KAEL:**
I assumed it was punishment.

**DOLEN:**
It was protection. The new structure—the Oversight Committee, the Auditors—they're watching you. You know too much about what happened. Classification keeps you close, lets them monitor you, but also...

*He hesitates.*

**DOLEN:**
Classification has its own secrets. Its own protections. Someone there has been shielding investigators for a long time. Mira was supposed to be transferred to Classification before they took her. It didn't happen in time.

**KAEL:**
Who's protecting me?

**DOLEN:**
I don't know. But they wanted you there. They've been waiting for someone like you.

*He turns to leave.*

**DOLEN:**
Be careful, K-7. The Registry has more layers than anyone knows. You've only scratched the surface.

*[IF TERMINATION PATH]:*

*[IF ACCELERATION PATH]:*
*The PURPOSE watches from the upper floors. A vast presence, patient and curious. Not hostile. Not safe. Just... there.*

*[IF CLASSIFICATION PATH]:*
*The building feels different now that KAEL has seen it from Floor 12. Deeper. Older. Full of doors that haven't been opened.*

*KAEL reaches home. Sits in the quiet.*

*On the table: a folder. The same folder Yemi Okonkwo hid in the Archives. KAEL kept a copy of the most important documents.*

*Inside, a page KAEL hasn't looked at closely before. A map—hand-drawn, old—of the Registry's lower levels. Subbasements 1, 2, 3...*

*And below Subbasement 3, unlabeled: a fourth level. With a door.*

*A note in Yemi's handwriting:*

```
The door has been locked since before the Charter.
What lies behind it is the reason for everything.
I never found the key.
Maybe you will.

— Y.O.
```

*KAEL stares at the map. At the door.*

*KAEL INTERNAL (Sardonic):*
> *One mystery solved. Ten more waiting. That's the Registry. That's always been the Registry.*

*KAEL INTERNAL (Diligent):*
> *A locked door. A missing key. This is where Season Two begins.*

*KAEL INTERNAL (Compassionate):*
> *Yemi tried. Mira tried. Others tried. They all fell short. But they left pieces behind. Enough pieces that someone, someday, might succeed.*

*KAEL INTERNAL (Complicit):*
> *I could stop here. File the map away. Forget the door. Go back to Classification and process souls and never ask another question.*

*Beat.*

*KAEL INTERNAL (all tones):*
> *But I won't. I never could. The questions are who I am now. The answers are somewhere in the depths.*
